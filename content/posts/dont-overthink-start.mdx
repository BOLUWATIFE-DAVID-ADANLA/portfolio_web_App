As a freelance developer and a junior mobile developer looking to break into corporate software engineering, one of the blockers I have — and still battle with — is overthinking my projects.

![Don't overthink, just start](/images/posts/1_z-QEU0ENwN4ZplutNxDjIQ.webp)

The mindset that everything has to be perfect and well thought out is a major factor in why many juniors (likewise myself) have little or no real-life practical projects on their portfolios.

If you are reading this article, let me guess — you are just like me. We want our apps to feel and flow smoothly, like enterprise-grade applications. I hate to be the bearer of bad news: they can't and they won't.

Many of the large companies you admire put months and large amounts of money into developing their apps. Can you compete even when you do not have the money or the expertise?

The answer is yes.

## Just Start Building

You might not have everything figured out yet — including the tech stack for your app — and that is okay.

Now, do not get me wrong. Planning is an essential part of the Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC). However, spending too much time trying to figure out every nitty-gritty detail of how your system should work can hold you back.

A developer once told me something that stuck:

> A crucial part of learning is lost if you are solely focused on finding the best way to do things.

Instead, build a proof of concept and keep iterating. After all, even the big players in the industry follow this approach — and so can you.

## The Lesson

I learned this the hard way, and I am now incorporating it into my workflow. The goal is not to build something perfect. The goal is to build something real — then make it better.

Start ugly. Iterate honestly. Ship anyway.

Happy building. 😊
